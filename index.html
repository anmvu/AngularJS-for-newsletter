In Memoriam: Eli Pearce

NYU Newsroom

When Professor Eli Pearce passed away on May 19, 2015, a slice of Poly history passed along with him. Pearce had been affiliated with the school, then known as the Polytechnic Institute of Brooklyn, since the mid-1950s, when he conducted his doctoral studies in chemistry here. As a student, he learned with such luminaries as Herman Frances Mark, who is often called the Father of Polymer Science, and Charles Overberger, another influential chemist who helped establish the study of polymers as a major sub-discipline.

25 Great Master of Financial Engineering Programs 2015

Master of Finance

5. Polytechnic Institute of New York University

The MS in Financial Engineering is a 33-credit program that can be completed either full-time or part-time. Students are required to complete an applied lab and a capstone experience which can be an internship, project or thesis. Students have the opportunity to complete the Bloomberg Essentials Online Training Program, which fulfills a requirement for graduation. The NYU School of Engineering has almost a 90% post-graduation job placement rate.

Game Changer: Game Maker & UX Designer Catt Small talks Multifaceted Skill-sets, Interactive Technology and Diverse Industries

The Mary Sue

Cat Small (MS, Integrated Digital Media) interview on the Code Liberation Foundation

It was a total accident! I just kept making games and talking to people about making games. Over time, I naturally grew a network of people and found myself attending many small and large industry events. At some point Phoenix Perry, a professor at NYU Polytechnic School of Engineering and maker of many things, took notice and asked me to help her create the Code Liberation Foundation, a group that teaches women to program video games. After I helped to start Code Liberation, people really started to pay attention. I also got better at making games through the process of teaching game development. Some say "those who can't, teach," but I think those who can should be the ones who teach.

How Tanya Menendez and Matthew Burnett, Founders of Maker’s Row, Spend Their Sundays

New York Times

Tanya Menendez and Matthew Burnett are the founders of Maker's Row, a Brooklyn start-up that connects entrepreneurs with American manufacturers. Forbes magazine recently included Ms. Menendez, 27, in its 30 under 30 issue. Mr. Burnett, 30, is a former watch designer who worked for Marc Jacobs and DKNY. Maker’s Row is their second company: In 2011, Ms. Menendez left a job at Goldman Sachs to join Mr. Burnett at a leather goods line he had started called the Brooklyn Bakery. A year before that, she joined him romantically. The two met through friends at a Roots concert in Prospect Park. They live in a one-bedroom apartment near Borough Hall in Downtown Brooklyn with their dog, Mr. Pickles.
